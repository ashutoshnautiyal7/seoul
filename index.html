Changdeokgung Palace
Changdeokgung Palace: A Tranquil Oasis Amidst Seoul's Bustle .

Nestled in the heart of Seoul, Changdeokgung Palace is a hidden gem that transports visitors to a bygone era of Korean royalty. Built during the Joseon Dynasty in 1405, the palace and its stunning Huwon (Secret Garden) are renowned for their harmonious integration with the natural landscape. As a UNESCO World Heritage site, Changdeokgung Palace captivates with its exquisite architecture, serene courtyards, and meandering pathways. Each hall and pavilion whispers tales of historical significance, making a visit to Changdeokgung an immersive journey into Korea's regal past.

National Museum of Korea
This is the largest and most popular museum in Korea with over 420,000 objects in its collection. It has six permanent exhibition galleries such as Calligraphy and Painting and the Room of Quiet Contemplation.

Situated in Yongsan, the National Museum of Korea stands as a custodian of the nation's rich cultural heritage. This vast institution houses an extensive collection of artifacts spanning millennia, showcasing the artistic, scientific, and historical achievements of Korea. From ancient relics to contemporary masterpieces, the museum provides a comprehensive narrative of the nation's journey. Exhibitions delve into diverse aspects of Korean life, fostering a deep appreciation for the country's traditions and innovations. A visit to the National Museum of Korea is a scholarly and visually stunning exploration of Korea's dynamic past.

N Seoul Tower
Built on a 262 meter peak in Namsan Park, the tower reaches to 480 meters above sea level. When the weather and pollution levels cooperate, visiting the observation tower (370 meters above sea level) allows you to view the entire city and surrounding areas.

Perched atop Namsan Mountain, N Seoul Tower is an iconic symbol of the city's skyline. Offering panoramic views of Seoul and beyond, this towering landmark is not just an observation deck but a cultural hub. Visitors can ascend the tower via cable car or a leisurely hike, reaching an observation deck adorned with colorful love locks and an array of restaurants. The tower's lights transform the night sky into a mesmerizing spectacle. N Seoul Tower is not only a panoramic vantage point but also a romantic destination, making it a must-visit for locals and tourists alike.

Cheonggyecheon Stream
Formerrly polluted and covered with an elevated road since 2005 this stream has been cleaned up and made into an Art and nature walkway through the heart of Seoul.

Cheonggyecheon Stream, flowing through the heart of Seoul, provides a serene escape from the urban hustle. Once covered by a highway, the restoration of Cheonggyecheon transformed it into a picturesque oasis. Lined with walking paths, bridges, and art installations, the stream invites locals and visitors to unwind amid nature's embrace. The sound of flowing water, the lush greenery, and the surrounding cityscape create a harmonious blend of urban and natural elements. Cheonggyecheon Stream exemplifies Seoul's commitment to preserving nature within its bustling metropolis, offering a peaceful retreat for contemplation and leisure.
